    • Welcome to RetaEnroll360! This is your system for managing your employees group insurance enrollment. Think of RetaEnroll360 as the engine that helps your manager the entire benefit plan life cycle for employees – from hire to retire and beyond

      With RetaEnroll360 you can easily & quickly do benefits administration such as*:

      • Add/Edit/Terminate employees
      • Add/Edit/Terminate employees’ dependents
      • Manage employees benefit elections/enrollments
      • Run more than 100 census, benefits/coverage, billing & more reports
      • Maintain notes about employees’ benefits and other HR transactions
      • Access premium billing invoices

      * Services may vary from description based on your contracted services

    • Who

      The Trustor purchases the group Medical insurance from the Reta Trust (“Reta”). As part of Reta's service, the Trustor provides RetaEnroll360 to help administrators like you and your employees manage benefit plan enrollment, premium billing/collection, compliance and other services as deemed appropriate by the Trustor.
      Reta Trust ("Reta") is non-profit employer trust that has provided affordable, quality healthcare for employees and members of Catholic organizations nationwide for over 40 years. Importantly, all Reta healthcare plans are designed to comply with the Ethical and Religious Directives of the Catholic Church.
      Benefit Allocation Systems ("BAS") is the developer and day-to-day administrator of RetaEnroll. BAS will provide you and your employees support for the use of and transactions associated with RetaEnroll360 . BAS provides a dedicated account manager to the HR/Benefits staff at the The Trustor, and provides a professional, friendly call center for location administrators and employees (See Getting Help for more information).

      What

      RetaEnroll360 is a private, secure, cloud-based software that Reta Trust provides to its clients like the Trustor, so that we have a best-in-class, online benefits enrollment & administration system. RetaEnroll360 is provided to you at no additional cost.

      When & Where

      RetaEnroll360 is cloud-based, so you and your employees will only need a standard Internet browser to access the system. In fact, in addition to working well on a personal computer/laptop, RetaEnroll360 is also designed to fit nicely on mobile devices; so, you'll be able to access RetaEnroll360 when and wherever you and your employees need access.

    • You, as a Master Administrator

      Your role within RetaEnroll360 is as a “Master Administrator.” This means that you, like other Master Administrators at The Trustor have the broadest access rights to the system.

    • Just an FYI...

      During your use of RetaEnroll360 and in communications we send you, you will see frequent references to the “Benefits Life Cycle” or BLC.

      The BLC refers to the journey an employee makes from the moment they are hired until the separate from the organization and all of the benefit-related transactions in-between, including but not limited to:

      • Add new hires
      • Communicate with new hires
      • New hire benefit plan enrollment
      • Managing employees' personnel data
      • Managing employees' dependents' data
      • Managing employee benefit plan elections
      • Employees' changes in status (e.g. Full-time to Part-time)
      • Employees' changes in salary
      • Employee termination/separation from service

    • Payroll Integration – PayCor to RetaEnroll360

      In order to keep employees’ data synchronized between our payroll system PayCor and RetaEnroll360 each week PayCor will provide a complete employee data file to RetaEnroll360 , then RetaEnroll360 will upload the PayCor data which in turn will update all employees information.

      he data PayCor provides RetaEnroll360 includes primarily employees’ personnel data including but not limited to:

      • Names
      • Home Addresses
      • Dates of Birth
      • Genders
      • Social Security Numbers
      • Salaries
      • Dates of Hire
      • Dates of Termination

      Payroll Integration - RetaEnroll360 to PayCor

      urther to synchronization, biweekly (before payroll runs), RetaEnroll360 will provide PayCor with a data file that includes all employees’ benefit elections ciated with a payroll deduction (i.e., employee contribution). PayCor will use this data to update employees benefit plan-related pay deductions that changed since the last data file exchange.

      Changing Employee' Personnel Data

      In order to keep the Paycor-BAS data synchronized; you will only be able to change employees’ personnel data in PayCor (RetaEnroll360 will be locked for such edits).

      Conversely, you will only be able to change employees’ benefits-related payroll deductions by making changes to their elections in RetaEnroll360

    • Managing Employees: Adds, Changes & Terminations

      Adds

      Employees can be added to RetaEnroll360 two different ways.

      1. Manual Process
        1. Employees can be manually added to RetaEnroll by utilizing the “Add New Employee Record” under the “Employee“ Menu Drop down. * Employee Validation required
      2. PayRoll File
        1. Employees can be added via a payroll file interchange. When this occurs, the payroll company system will act as the system of record. All changes must be made in the payroll system.

      *Reta requires all new hires go through a verification process to validate they are an eligible employee. When an employee is manually added, RetaEnroll will prompt you for the required employee documentation. A list of acceptable documents will be presented when adding a new hire. If an employee is added by way of a file import (e.g. Payroll file), validation documentation will not be required as the file is the validation.

      Changes

      You may manage employees’ dependents, enrollments, and certain personnel data not locked by the payroll data exchange process.

      Changes to employees’ enrollment - either changes in benefit options/plan levels and/or the addition of dependents may be made as follows:

      New Hire Open Enrollment Period - Add/Drop/Change optional benefits for the employee & dependents Mid-Year Life Event (the period following in between New Hire Open Enrollment and Annual Open Enrollment) may only occur coincident with a Permissible life event. The system will control these changes and prompt you for supporting documentation, as necessary.

      Terminations

      You can only make an employee termination in the PayCor system. PayCor will notify RetaEnroll of your employee terminations.

    • Managing Dependents: Adds, Changes, & Terminations

      Adds

      Dependents can be added to RetaEnroll 360 during any enrollment period.

      *Reta requires all new covered dependents go through a verification process to validate they are an eligible dependent. When a dependent is added to coverage, RetaEnroll will prompt the employee for the required dependent documentation. A list of acceptable documents will be presented when adding a new dependent to coverage. Employee has 30 days from the start of their enrollment to upload the required dependent documentation. If the enrollment window has closed, employee can still upload the required dependent documentation by utilizing the “Manage Dependents Verifications” under the Employee Menu.

      Changes

      Employees can manage their dependents coverage and demographics information during any enrollment period.

      Terminations

      Employee dependents can be removed from coverage during an enrollment period and if an employee dependent reaches the age limit of 26, RetaEnroll will terminate the dependent and offer COBRA to the overage dependent effective the 1st of the month after their 26th birthday.

    • Managing Employee Terminations

      Employees can be terminated in RetaEnroll360 two different ways

      1. Manual Termination

        1. Employees can be manually terminated by a master administrator or any applicable location administrator initializing the “Terminate Employee” menu item under the “Employee “Menu Drop down. You will then be walked through the termination wizard and offer COBRA based on the applicable termination reason.

      2. Payroll File

        1. Employees can be terminated via a payroll file interchange. When this occurs, the payroll company system will act as the system of record. All changes must be made in the payroll system. The payroll file will also prompt a COBRA letter to be generated based on the information provided on the applicable file.

      If Cobra was offered to the employee, the administrator who processed the termination will receive a confirmation email summarizing the termination that was processed which will include the COBRA details.

      All Cobra letters are printed and mailed the following day.

    • Premium Invoices

      Trustor Invoices

      Trustor invoices are available directly on RetaEnroll on the 21st of each month under the “Billing” section located under the Administrator menu or under the Quick link section labeled “Billing” on the Master or Location Administrator homepage. Reta bills a month in advance, (e.g. September invoice posted on September 21st will be for the month of October). All changes prior to the 19th of each month will be applied to the applicable location invoice. All changes after the 19th will be reflective on the following months invoice.

      Location Invoices

      Location invoices are available directly on RetaEnroll on the 21st of each month under the “Billing” section located under the Administrator menu or under the Quick link section labeled “Billing” on the Master or Location Administrator homepage. Reta bills a month in advance, (e.g. September invoice posted on September 21st will be for the month of October). All changes prior to the 19th of each month will be applied to the applicable location invoice. All changes after the 19th will be reflective on the following months invoice.

      If you are also setup as a “Billing Contact”, you will also receive a emailed copy of your applicable location invoice(s). There is only one billing contact associated with each location. The name listed on the top of the invoice generally will reflect the main “Billing Contact” name.

      Retroactive Changes

      When changes occur and retroactive credit or charges are applicable, the applicable monthly location invoice will indicate these values in the “Adjustments” section of the location invoice.

      Invoice History

      All historical invoices are available on RetaEnroll 24/7 365 under the “Billing” section located under the Administrator menu or by way of “Quick link” section on the Administrator homepage.

      Billing Reporting

      Aging Report - An accounts receivable aging is a report that lists unpaid Location invoices. The aging report is the primary tool used by collections personnel to determine which location invoices are overdue for payment. This reporting can be located under the billing section.

    • Managing Administrator Rights

      Adding a new Administrator or requesting Administrator Rights.

      Paper Form

      All new Location Administrators must complete an “Administrator request form”. A completed administrator form must be signed by either your locations current administrator or the Master contact.

      Billing Contact

      Each location has one assigned billing contact, this administrator is the name listed on the monthly invoice. If you are adding or removing an administrator, please also notify RetaEnroll if your new contact or old contact needs to be modified as the main billing contact.

      Removing Administrator Access

      Option 1: If you are replacing a current/terminated administrator and would like to ensure their access has been terminated, please complete the removal/replace section on the “Administrator request form”. If someone is replacing the user you are removing access for, please include their information on the same form.

      Option 2: Please reach out directly to your dedicated BAS AM or our client service by way of email with the administrators name and location.

      Option 3: If you are utilizing the Manage Administrator application, you can remove access directly in the application.
